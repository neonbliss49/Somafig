Design a premium iOS mobile app experience for a health intelligence product called "Somatiq".

This is NOT a static screen.
Create a multi-state interactive flow that can be prototyped in Figma.

---

GOAL:
Simulate an interactive “Today” experience where the user:

1. Sees an insight
2. Reveals the cause
3. Makes a decision
4. Sees a personalized response

---

TARGET USER:
Women 35–55 dealing with fatigue, hormonal imbalance, and unpredictable energy.

Tone:
Calm, intelligent, premium, emotionally reassuring.

---

CREATE 4 SEPARATE SCREENS (STATES):

---

### SCREEN 1 — HERO (Collapsed State)

Content:

* Header:
  "Good Morning, Hanna"

* Main Insight (large typography):
  "Your body is entering a low-recovery phase today"

* Subtle CTA:
  "See what’s driving this ↓"

Design:

* Clean, minimal
* Strong typography hierarchy
* Soft background (light gradient or blurred nature)

---

### SCREEN 2 — CAUSE REVEAL

Triggered by tap.

Content:

* Same header + insight (smaller now)
* Visual causal chain (IMPORTANT):

Sleep ↓ → HRV ↓ → Energy ↓

* Luteal phase (Day 18)

- Supporting text:
  "Driven by 3 short nights and rising temperature"

- Pattern validation:
  "Seen in 4 of your last 6 similar patterns"

Design:

* Use visual connection (lines or flow)
* Elements appear structured and connected
* Avoid bullet list feeling

---

### SCREEN 3 — DECISION STATE (CRITICAL)

Content:

* Question:
  "How do you want to approach today?"

* Two large selectable options:

[ Conserve energy ]
[ Push through ]

Design:

* Buttons should feel like choices, not generic CTAs
* Calm but distinct styles

---

### SCREEN 4 — RESPONSE STATE (after selection)

If "Conserve energy" selected:

Content:

* Recommendation:
  • Reduce load by ~30%
  • Magnesium at 21:00
  • Sleep before 23:00

* Proof:
  "Magnesium improved your deep sleep in 3 of your last 7 similar patterns (+25 min)"

* Future:
  "Energy dip likely continues tomorrow. Recovery peak expected Monday."

Design:

* Slight visual shift to confirm selection
* Calm highlight of chosen path

---

VISUAL STYLE:

* Premium, calm, minimal
* Soft shadows, subtle depth
* No dashboards, no clutter
* Avoid too many cards
* Strong typography hierarchy

---

PROTOTYPE INSTRUCTIONS:

* Link Screen 1 → Screen 2 on tap ("See what’s driving this")
* Link Screen 2 → Screen 3 (auto or tap)
* Link Screen 3 → Screen 4 based on selection
* Use "Smart Animate" for smooth transitions

---

IMPORTANT:

Do NOT create a static health dashboard.
Create a guided, interactive experience.

The UI should feel like:
→ insight is revealed step by step
→ the user participates in understanding

---

The result should feel:
intelligent, personal, and slightly magical.
